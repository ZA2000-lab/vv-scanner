Strategy: Cash-Secured Put (CSP) — The Wheel
Sell an out-of-the-money put at ~30-delta with 30–50 DTE when IV rank is elevated (>50%). Collect premium now, buy stock at a discount if assigned. Then sell covered calls — repeat.
Three Entry Conditions
›STRONG — IV Rank ≥ 60%Options are rich relative to 1-year realized vol. Best premium collection environment.
›DECENT — IV Rank 45–59%Acceptable premium. Verify technical support at the strike before entering.
›SKIP — IV Rank < 45%Premium too thin relative to risk. Earnings within expiry = automatic SKIP.
Trade Execution
›StructureSell to Open 1+ put contract(s). Cash-secured: keep full collateral (strike × 100) in account.
›Strike~30-delta put. Out-of-the-money, typically 5–15% below current price. Should be near technical support.
›Expiry30–50 DTE. Sweet spot for theta decay without excessive overnight-event risk.
›Take ProfitClose at 50% of premium collected. Do not hold to expiry.
›Stop LossClose at 2× premium paid (200% loss on position).
The Wheel (If Assigned)
›Layer 3: You now own 100 shares at the put strike. Your effective cost basis = strike − premium collected.
›Immediately sell a covered call at or slightly above your cost basis, 30–45 DTE. Collect more premium.
›Repeat monthly. Each cycle lowers your breakeven by 1–3%. Stock called away = full profit on the trade cycle.
Critical Rules
›EarningsNEVER sell a CSP that expires after an earnings date. Earnings = gap risk. Skip and re-enter post-earnings.
›SpreadBid/ask spread > 15% = avoid. Wide spreads mean poor fills and illiquid options.
›SupportAlways verify the strike is near a chart support level. IV rank is not a substitute for technical analysis.
›SizingMax 5–10% of portfolio per position. Collateral requirements limit natural position sizing.
